MISSION

Unity Center seeks to build hope and opportunity by promoting educational, social and spiritual growth for all.

Unity Center was established in June of 1998 as an outreach ministry of Broad Street United Methodist Church, to build community in East Cleveland. Unity Center firmly believes in equality and therefore welcomes students of various races, ethnicities, and religious and non-religious affiliations. The FUTURE After School Program (FUTURE - Fun at Unity Totally United Reaching for Excellence) is a primary program of Unity Center. It began to provide students in grades K-5 with a safe, caring place to play, learn life skills, enjoy enrichment activities and receive academic assistance. As youth desired to continue to attend as middle and high schoolers, a comprehensive Youth Leadership Development Program was initiated to assist students in 7th grade and beyond in realizing and using their leadership skills both among their peers and within the context of their larger communities. Building a diverse and supportive community of love and nurture continues for both students and families.

PROGRAM GROWTH

In 1999, Unity Center began to offer summer day camps and the FUTURE 2-day per week after school program with an average of 15 students.

2000-2005: 1st Community Egg Hunt, 1st Community Choir Program, 1st Skating Party, 1st Teacher Tea, Tutor Training Program began, added 3rd Day, Wonders in the Woods Unity Tent Camps began, renovated sanctuary for usable student space, average attendance 45

2006-2010: Renovation of Basement and upstairs bathrooms and entrance of Community Building, 1st Dress for Success, 1st Youth Leader trip to Washington DC, acquired adjoining property for development, added piano lab, DAWN Youth group organized, began affiliation with Lee University to utilize students as tutors, added 4th day, average attendance 60

2011-2016: added snack preparation volunteers, became members of TIRRC (Tennessee Immigration Refugee Rights Coalition), renovated Melting Pot for office space and youth and adult programming, opened playfield, built addition onto Home Place (commercial kitchen, accessible bathrooms, storage), 1st Tellus Science Museum Trip for 3rd graders, average attendance 90

2017-Present: Purchased and renovated adjoining property and house "House of Dreams" for expanded programing, began partnership with United Way of Ocoee Region and AmeriCorps VISTA program, 1st Nashville Trek Enrichment trip for 4th graders, began Story Catchers program, average attendance 92

DREAMS for TOMORROW

As the Unity Center Community has developed and students graduate, have their own families and return, the challenge of meeting the needs of the future lies before us. Goals of improving our facilities to make them more safe and secure; adding a fifth day to the after school program and even launching satellite centers in the greater Cleveland community will enable this outreach ministry to continue its impact.
